analysis 1 — Mbappé involvement

France are a completely different attacking team with Mbappé involved

Sequences involving Mbappé produce —× more shots per sequence and —× more goals per sequence than those without him. Lines broken per sequence is also dramatically higher with his involvement. This is the single biggest tactical dependency in the dataset.

Pass delivery to Mbappé degrades when France chase goals

When France are behind, passes aimed at Mbappé succeed at 71%, which is slightly better than after France score (67%), but his own pass completion drops to 76% (vs 88–90% in other phases). Under pressure he is receiving less cleanly and passing less accurately. This is the data signature of isolation rather than connection.

Without Mbappé, France have almost no attacking threat

Sequences without Mbappé involvement average 0.014–0.058 shots per sequence across games, versus 0.143–0.348 with him. In the Tunisia and Argentina games, where France struggled most, the without-Mbappé sequences were the least productive of the tournament. France's attacking output is highly concentrated in one player.

Implication: neutralising Mbappé neutralises France

Morocco (0-0 until 79') and Tunisia (0-0 for whole regulation) both showed that when Mbappé is held without involvement, France's without-Mbappé sequences do not compensate. The pass network data shows France do not have a reliable alternative attacking route.

analysis 2 — shot quality by phase

Mbappé is most lethal when chasing: 42% goal rate after conceding

After the opponent scores, Mbappé converts 42% of his shots into goals (3 goals from 7 shots). This is higher than after France score (25%, 4 from 16) or at game start (14%, 1 from 7). His individual quality actually rises under pressure; the team's problem is structural, not Mbappé's personal form.

Most shots come after France score, but conversion is lower

16 of Mbappé's 30 shots came in after-France-goal phases. Despite the volume, conversion was only 25% and 68% of those shots came under pressure, the highest pressed rate of any phase. France may be playing faster and more directly after scoring rather than building cleaner chances for him.

analysis 3 — passing network

Tchouaméni is France's central hub: but his targeting shifts when chasing

Tchouaméni is the #1 pass distributor in all three phase types, accounting for 10–14% of all completed pass involvement. Critically, when France are chasing goals he targets Mbappé 15 times (vs 12 in game start, becoming his 2nd-most-targeted player); but the Tchouaméni→Mbappé completion rate falls when the pressure is highest.

Griezmann becomes more central after France score, a tactical strength

In after-France-goal phases, Griezmann rises from 6th to 3rd in pass involvement. He links play more actively when France control the game. His involvement in calmer phases correlates with France's safest tactical periods. The network becomes more balanced and less Mbappé-dependent when ahead.

When chasing, Varane and Kounde drop in involvement, and the wide defenders recede

Varane drops from 2nd overall (game start) to 9th (after opp goal). Kounde drops from 5th to 8th. When France need a goal, the full-backs and centre-backs contribute less to build-up. France become more vertical and central, bypassing the wide defensive outlets. This compresses the attack and makes them more predictable.

Camavinga enters the network when chasing, a deliberate adjustment

Camavinga appears in the top 10 only in after-opponent-goal phases (4th among non-starters, 10th overall), reflecting his role as an impact substitute when France need urgency. His presence shifts the network toward more central distribution. Whether this is sufficiently effective is not clear from pass counts alone.
