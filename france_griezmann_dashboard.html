What the data shows about Griezmann's role

France are 3.6x more dangerous per sequence with Griezmann directly involved

Sequences where Griezmann passes, shoots, carries or dribbles produce 3.6x more shots and 1.5x more goals per sequence than those without him. He is involved in 19% of all France attacking sequences. Unlike Mbappe (8.6x multiplier, finisher role), Griezmann's multiplier reflects his role as the connector who creates the conditions for others to shoot - not someone who shoots himself.

His passing is the most reliable in the squad across all game states

Griezmann completes 77% of passes at game start, 89% after France score, and 82% when chasing goals. The drop when chasing is real but modest - he remains accurate under pressure. His completion rate in Griezmann-involved sequences is also higher than in non-Griezmann sequences in every single game.

Mbappe is his primary pass target - the Griezmann to Mbappe link is France's most important creative chain

Griezmann targets Mbappe 17 times at game start (tournament total: 48 passes at 75% completion). Tchouameni feeds Griezmann most (94% completion), creating the key triangle: Tchouameni to Griezmann to Mbappe. When Griezmann is unavailable to play this link, Tchouameni must target Mbappe directly at lower completion rates.

He is France's most active press trigger from the front - Mbappe barely presses at all

Griezmann accounts for 12% of France's pressing at game start, rising to 13% after France score. His best pressing games are Morocco (18%) and Australia (17%). Mbappe by comparison contributes only 4-8% depending on phase. Griezmann is the engine of France's high press - without him their defensive structure begins higher up the pitch would be fundamentally different.

Argentina: when Griezmann is removed, France lose their creative structure

Against Argentina, Griezmann was substituted at 98'. His press share in that game was only 8% - his lowest - reflecting that France were largely in reactive mode for much of the match. After his substitution France's passing became more direct with lower completion, and they relied almost entirely on individual Mbappe moments rather than structured build-up through the midfield.
