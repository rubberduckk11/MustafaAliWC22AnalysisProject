Analysis 1 - Mbappe involvement

France are 6.9x more dangerous per sequence with Mbappe directly involved

Sequences where Mbappe passes, shoots, carries or dribbles produce 6.9x more shots and 33.0x more goals per sequence. He is involved in 18% of all France attacking sequences - the single biggest tactical dependency in the data.

Pass delivery to Mbappe degrades when France chase goals

When France are behind, passes aimed at Mbappe reach him at 71% (vs 73% at game start). His own pass completion drops to 77% when chasing (vs 91% at game start). The data signature of isolation under pressure.

Analysis 2 - Shot quality

Mbappe converts 43% of shots when chasing - his highest phase

After the opponent scores: 43% conversion (3 goals from 7 shots). Higher than after France score (25%) or game start (14%). Individual quality rises under pressure even as team structure deteriorates.

Most shots come after France score but conversion is lower

16 of 30 total shots came in after-France-goal phases at only 25% conversion and 69% pressed - the highest pressed rate of any phase.

Analysis 3 - Passing network

Tchouameni is France's central hub and targeting shifts when chasing

Tchouameni is the #1 pass distributor in all phase types. When France are behind he targets Mbappe more (17 passes to his top target) but the network becomes more direct and less balanced overall.

Griezmann becomes more central after France score

In after-France-goal phases Griezmann rises in pass involvement. The network is more balanced through more players when France control the game - correlating with their safest tactical periods.
